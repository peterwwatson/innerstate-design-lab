Breaths lower CO₂ → the breathe-now alarm goes quiet.

Take ~30 full breaths — belly then chest, let the exhale drop. Within a minute the hands tingle: that's lowered CO₂ changing nerve excitability. Normal, and a sign it's working.

Lineage-teaching voice

Settle — hold the mind on the breath

Underneath the chemistry is the instruction that matters most in the long run: keep attention on the breath; don't get lost in thought. The inward turn begins here.

A body relaxed enough to let go can store and settle what a tense body can't. Relaxing is treated as a skill, not a mood.

Exhale and hold empty. The stillness surprises you — but your oxygen is actually falling. The calm isn't stored air; it's that the CO₂ alarm hasn't switched on yet.

Lineage-teaching voice

Release — thoughts arise as stress leaves

In the hold, thoughts come. The teaching reframes them: stress leaving the body generates those thoughts — they're the practice working, not failing.

Noticing you've drifted and returning to the breath — that return is the practice itself. A learning and a purifying, one return at a time.

The same move, every lineage

Wander, notice, return. Only the vehicle differs — breath here, mantra elsewhere. See row 05.

03Recover·Stillness

Recover on the honest signal

CO₂ rebuilds; the urge returns — that returning pull is your body's honest cue. One full inhale, held ~15 s with attention drawn gently up toward the head, eyes soft and open, then release everything.

"Feel the pressure, don't force it — then let it all go."
Lineage-teaching voice

Stillness — the inward-facing quiet

What's left when you release is the point of the round: a settled, cleansed quiet with nothing to do. In the teaching this isn't a side effect — it's the doorway the whole round was travelling toward.

After the final recovery, no more breath control — just noticing the settled, cleansed state. The discipline delivers you to the door; the sitting is what you do there. With time the foreground becomes stillness, not sensation.

How far to trust each part

The bold claims sit on a ladder of evidence, not a single yes-or-no. Each rung labelled by tier — nothing dismissed for being hard to measure, nothing sold as medicine.

Measured in trials
Fast breathing lowers CO₂ and mutes air hunger; practitioners blunted an inflammatory response (IL-6/TNF down ~50%). Oxygen falls in the hold — not raised.
n=24 healthy men, one lab session (Kox 2014, PNAS). Confirmed within scope.
Measured in trials
Over 3 weeks it eased depression and anxiety — but about equally to slow breathing; its edge was less post-stress rumination.
n=84 depressed beginners, 3 weeks (Blades 2024). Not a verdict on full intent over years.
Demonstrated feat
Core temperature held within 0.7 °C through 88 minutes of full-body ice immersion, no shivering, heat ~doubled.
n-of-1, monitored (Bongers 2019). Mechanism partly open. NOT safe for an untrained person to attempt.
Mass testimony
Across large populations the same reports recur: energy, clarity, calmer stress, connection — learnable in one sitting, free at home.
Peer-reviewed practitioner studies (Huish 2025; King 2025). Value and accessibility, not mechanism. Heavy selection effects.
First-person
The owner reports full-intent breathing has stopped tooth pain and cleared infection in one program.
n-of-1, uncontrolled. Critical variable: intent — the one thing no trial controls.
Frontier · open
There may be an energetic dimension current instruments don't capture — "they do not know how" to measure it.
Explicitly open. The honest path: build the instrument the studies lack, and test it in your own body.

⚠︎ Read before you practise

  • Never in or near water — breath-holds cause shallow-water blackout; documented drownings, including practitioners.
  • Never driving or standing. Always seated or lying down; fainting can come without warning.
  • Tingling, light-headedness, hand cramping are common and harmless — ease off and breathe normally.
  • Don't force the hold; let the natural urge to breathe signal recovery.
  • Skip or consult a professional if pregnant, or with epilepsy, heart conditions, high blood pressure, fainting history, or panic disorder.
  • A wellness practice, not medical treatment. "Healer / alchemist" is inspiration, not a cure.

Breathe, let go, hold, recover, and rest in a calm, open, inward-facing state you can return to anytime. The left column is real and modest; the right is the teaching's own map; the deeper claims are held honestly, by tier. If you want to know how far it goes — test it in your own body.
