See it before you feel it

Breathe, let go, and cross into a stillness you can return to.

A rhythm you can learn in one sitting: ~30 full breaths, hold empty, recover, repeat. The chemistry explains why the hold feels calm — but the practice is really training two things at once, a body skill and an inward path. This page shows both, side by side, honestly labelled.

Inner · where it takes you

Settle — hold the mind on the breath

The instruction underneath the chemistry: keep your attention on the breath and don't get lost in thoughts. This is the beginning of the inward turn.

Simple, but not easy — and that difficulty is the training, not an obstacle to it.

The teaching's frame: a body relaxed enough to let go can store what a tense body can't.

Inner · where it takes you

Release — thoughts arise as stress leaves

In the hold, thoughts surface. The teaching reframes them: stress released from the body generates thoughts. They aren't failure — they're the practice working.

Noticing you've been carried off, and returning to the breath — that turning-back is the practice itself. A learning and a purifying, one return at a time.

Why this is the whole game

Every school of this shape trains the same move: wander, notice, return. The vehicle differs; the return is universal. See the inner track's final station.

03Recover · Stillness

When the urge honestly returns, you recover — and drop into a settled quiet.

Outer · the honest signal

One recovery breath, held with a light squeeze

CO₂ rebuilds; the urge returns — that returning pull is your body's honest cue to recover. Take one full inhale, hold ~15 s with attention drawn gently up toward the head, eyes soft and open, then let it all go.

During the recovery hold
Head pressure, warmth, a wave of relief
"Feel the pressure, don't force it — then let it all go."

Feels like: fullness in head and chest, then release.

Inner · where it takes you

Stillness — the inward-facing quiet

After recovery, rest. No breath to control, nothing to do. This is the settled, cleansed state the whole round was travelling toward — the doorway to the inner track's deeper stations.

A calm, open, inward-facing state you can learn to return to anytime.

⚠︎ Read before you practise

  • Never in or near water. Breath-holds cause shallow-water blackout — there are documented drownings, including practitioners.
  • Never while driving or standing. Always seated or lying down; fainting can happen without warning and is common enough to expect.
  • Tingling, light-headedness, and hand cramping (tetany) are common and harmless — ease off and breathe normally.
  • Do not force the hold; let the natural urge to breathe be your signal to recover.
  • Skip or consult a professional if pregnant, or with epilepsy, cardiovascular conditions, high blood pressure, a history of fainting, or panic disorder.
  • This is a wellness practice, not medical treatment. "Healer / alchemist / own 100% of your brain" is inspiration, not a cure.

What's real and repeatable is simpler than the legend.

Breathe, let go, hold, recover, and rest in a calm, open, inward-facing state you can return to anytime.
