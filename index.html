Explainer design lab · Round 1

Four competing "See it" pages — same content, four different souls.

All four are built from the approved Wim Hof pack (WP-V1.3) plus the mocked inner-path material the pack still lacks. Every one honors the founding critique: a persistent dual-track journey map (outer discipline · inner purpose) with a "you are here" that moves as you scroll, the purpose dimension as a first-class, honestly-tiered voice, and the §2 concept pictures — causal chain, false-alarm gauge, the bridge, the progression ladder. Dark surfaces only.

Outer track — the discipline Inner track — the purpose Judge them side by side, then critique.
Candidate A

Map-led

The journey map is the page spine. A persistent dual-track rail runs down the left; every step of content hangs off a station where outer and inner sit as two cards, side by side.
  • Optimizes for: never losing the whole arc; structural clarity
  • Outer & inner given equal visual weight at every step
  • Risk: the twin-card rhythm can feel systematic
Metaphor: a trail map with two parallel paths

Candidate B

Story-led

A narrative walk through one session — "sit down, here's what happens." Prose carries you; concept pictures interrupt it; the map shrinks to a slim margin companion. Purpose surfaces as inline asides.
  • Optimizes for: emotional pull, readability, momentum
  • The inner path arrives as the story's own turn
  • Risk: less scannable; asks for reading attention
Metaphor: a guided first-person voice, map in the wings
Open Candidate B →
Candidate C

Two-column parallel

A persistent split screen: discipline on the left, meaning on the right, the whole way down. A sticky dual-track header shows position on both tracks at once. Same step, two truths, same altitude.
  • Optimizes for: the "two dimensions together" thesis, literally
  • Forces constant comparison of body-move and inner-move
  • Risk: two columns can compete for the eye
Metaphor: a parallel text — physiology & scripture facing pages
Open Candidate C →
Candidate D

Depth-led

One hero picture per screen, five-word caption, everything else folded into drill-downs. Full-viewport panels; a compact dual-track dot-map on the edge. Simple on the surface, deep on demand.
  • Optimizes for: "a picture worth a thousand words"; calm focus
  • Complexity absorbed — the reader chooses their depth
  • Risk: depth is hidden; a skimmer may miss the tiers
Metaphor: a slide deck you can pause and open
Open Candidate D →
How to read these: open each, scroll top to bottom, and watch the two-track map move. Ask of each — does the inner path feel first-class and honest (not a weak claim, not mocked, not footnoted away)? Do the pictures predict a feeling? Is the discipline/purpose pairing legible? Then pick the direction that pulls you in, and note what you'd keep from the others. Your read decides which one gets built.
